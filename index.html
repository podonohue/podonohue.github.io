Introduction

This User Manual is intended to give you some idea of how I work best. It's a living document describing some facets of my personality, created with the intent of increasing understanding and collaboration.

It's not a list of demands or commandments on how to interact with me. I'm highly adaptable and collaborate with empathy - we'll do great things together.

Ideal Conditions

  • Caffeinated & well-rested.
  • Music: lo-fi, chill house, instrumental, sigur ros - something without intelligible lyrics to distract me (aka make me want to sing along).
  • When I need to focus, I'm most productive on my own in a relatively quiet space (or: with headphones). Otherwise, I keep an open door policy for my team.

Mornings

Brief check-in with the team, then knock out my dailies, then work on projects.

Apply two cups of coffee, then switch to decaf tea so I don't lose molecular cohesion.

I typically schedule a two-hour block, 2-3 days each week for deep focus.

Afternoons

Afternoons best for collaboration. This is when most of my 1:1's take place, as well as project updates and group initiatives.

Nights

Most evenings I'll do a short review of standard and high priority items, but I typically save the heavy lifting for the next morning. I'm a believer in Work/Life Alignment - there is a real return on investing in downtime and ensuring my work and leisure benefit one another.

Communication

  • Low to Medium Priority: direct message.
  • High Priority or Sensitive Topics: call/video to help ensure clarity of tone.

Style

  • I do best with well-communicated deadlines and goals. I use the pressure to make diamonds.
  • I learn through visuals and by doing: exercises, experimentation, reading, graphics, demonstrations.
  • I have strong opinions, but I'm open to new facts.
  • I aim to put a lot of heart into my work, minus ego. I enjoy discussing opposing viewpoints and playing devil's advocate. It's OK to tell me "Please stop debating and start collaborating."
  • I strongly prefer clear and direct communication.

What I Do Not Care For

  • Ambiguity and obfuscation. I can become impatient or unfocused if the person talking to me is dancing around the point or over-explaining.
  • Complaints without action. A gripe session may be a bonding/validating experience, but doesn't do anything to make the situation better.
  • Stubbornly holding onto tradition despite new facts (a.k.a. "that's the way we've always done it").

What Inspires Me

  • Elegant solutions - maximum effect from simplest effort.
  • Taking initiative to solve problems and improve the lives/work of those around you.
  • Open-mindedness to new opportunities to grow your knowledge/skills/expertise/life experience.
  • Leaving things better than you found them.
